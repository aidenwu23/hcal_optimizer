\begin{abstract}
\noindent \textit{This note studies whether low-energy neutron detection in the ePIC nHCal can be improved within a geometry whose total depth remains in a comparable range through segment-dependent
absorber and scintillator thickness variation across three longitudinal segments. Geometries were evaluated with DD4hep/DDsim using a neutron G4GPS beam specification over a continuous
momentum range of 0.1--2.5 GeV/c, and ranked with a surrogate-guided optimization loop. Detection efficiency, average fired-tile count, and average fired-layer count were used
to compare candidate geometries. The optimization landscape was found to be shallow: the best-performing geometry was already present in the initial 150-point Latin-hypercube scan,
and later surrogate-guided proposals did not consistently outperform it after higher-stat reruns. The final optimum showed a modest improvement over the baseline nHCal geometry.
}
\end{abstract}
